# Community & Ecosystem

<page_summary>
  The dstack community represents a convergence of security researchers, cloud architects, cryptographers, and systems engineers united by a shared vision of democratizing confidential computing. This page serves as the authoritative guide to engaging with our ecosystem‚Äîwhether seeking technical assistance, contributing code, participating in governance, or advancing the state of secure computing. Our community operates on principles of transparency, technical excellence, and collaborative innovation.
</page_summary>

<smoothly_flowing_prose_paragraphs>
  Open-source projects succeed not merely through code quality but through the strength of their communities. The dstack ecosystem exemplifies this principle, bringing together diverse perspectives from academia, industry, and independent researchers. Our community's unique value proposition lies in bridging the gap between theoretical cryptographic research and practical deployment challenges, creating a feedback loop that benefits both domains.

  The transition from traditional computing to confidential computing requires more than technical innovation‚Äîit demands cultural change, educational resources, and collaborative problem-solving. The dstack community serves as the catalyst for this transformation, providing forums for knowledge exchange, collaborative development, and collective governance of the platform's evolution.
</smoothly_flowing_prose_paragraphs>

### Participation Levels

<Highlight>
  Community members progress through contribution levels, gaining increased governance rights and responsibilities.
</Highlight>

1. **Observer**: Read access, community participation
2. **Contributor**: Code/documentation submissions, issue creation
3. **Committer**: Direct repository access, review rights
4. **Maintainer**: Subsystem ownership, architectural decisions
5. **Steering Committee**: Platform-wide governance
